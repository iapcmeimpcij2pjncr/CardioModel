Healthy Heart Model

Explore the Healthy Heart Model in CardioModel, an interactive Python GUI that simulates normal cardiovascular function using a biventricular finite element (FE) model coupled with coronary perfusion in a closed-loop systemic and pulmonary circulation. This baseline model captures myocardial demand-supply feedback, including metabolic regulation and myocardium-vessel interactions, under resting and exercise conditions, reproducing pressure-volume loops, ejection fractions, and regional blood flows in a healthy heart. Calibrated with clinical data, it highlights how a normal heart maintains homeostasis against increased demand, such as through efficient pumping from the ventricles, proper valve function to direct blood flow, and a steady electrical system regulating heartbeat at 60 to 100 beats per minute, contrasting with pathologies like coronary artery disease involving plaque buildup in arteries, heart failure where the heart cannot pump effectively, arrhythmias disrupting heart rhythm, and congenital defects like atrial septal defects. Users can adjust parameters to visualize hemodynamics, stresses, and perfusion, providing insights into preventive strategies against major cardiovascular diseases, which include heart attacks and strokes as leading global causes of death.
